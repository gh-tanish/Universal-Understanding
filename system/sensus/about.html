EXPERIENCE IN THE UNIVERSE

Understanding reality through awareness and experience.

ETYMOLOGY

Sensus: from Latin sēnsus, meaning “perception,” “feeling,” “awareness,” or “sense,” derived from sentīre — “to perceive.”

The Purpose of Sensus

Sensus aims to structure the study of mind and perception into a coherent framework — showing how awareness emerges, processes information, and constructs experience from sensory input.

Beyond Subjectivity

Rather than treating consciousness as purely philosophical or abstract, Sensus explores it as a system — with layers of cognition, perception, interpretation, and feedback.

Core Principles

Cognition & Psychology

The study of the mind, perception, and human experience. Exploring cognition, consciousness, behaviour, and how psychological processes shape how we think and understand the world.

Sociology

The study of social systems, collective behaviour, and human organisation. Exploring structures, politics, and how societies influence individuals.

Anthropology & Linguistics

The study of humanity through culture, language, and development. Anthropology explores human societies, while linguistics examines systems of language.

Humanities

The study of human thought, belief, and history. Exploring philosophy, ethics, religion, and how ideas shape civilisation over time.
